Symptoms of a brain tumour

The symptoms of a brain tumour vary depending on the exact part of the brain affected.

Common symptoms include:(Headaches, Seizures, Vision or Speech problems and Progressive weakness).

When to see GP

See a GP if you have these types of symptoms, You may not have a brain tumour.

but these types of symptoms should be checked, such as a brain scan.

Giloma

Gliomas are brain tumours that start in glial cells. These are the supporting cells of the brain and the spinal cord. The most common type is called astrocytoma.

Meningioma

Meningiomas are tumours that start in the layers of tissue (meninges) that cover the brain and spinal cord. Most meningiomas are benign.

Pituitary tumours tumours

The pituitary gland makes and releases hormones into the bloodstream. Most pituitary tumours are benign. Benign pituitary gland tumours are also called pituitary adenomas.
